Fees vs. Pass Rate (Scatter)
Annual fees (GHS) plotted against BECE pass rate per school

A moderate positive correlation exists between annual fees and pass rates (r ≈ 0.74). Schools charging above ₵380/year consistently achieve pass rates above 63%, suggesting that resource availability — funded by fees — significantly influences outcomes.

However, this also exposes an equity gap: schools serving the poorest communities charge less but lack the resources needed to achieve comparable results without external support.

Infrastructure Score by Region
Facility quality index (0–100) — colour coded by threshold

5 of 10 schools score below 45/100 — classified as "Critical". Upper East (30) and Upper West (32) rank lowest, lacking functional toilets, libraries, and adequate classroom space per pupil.

Infrastructure score below 40 correlates strongly with pass rates under 55%, reinforcing the need for capital grants to underserved regions.

Analysis & Recommendations
Evidence-Based Policy Insights
Key findings from school-level data, mapped to Ghana Education Service SDG action priorities.
SDG 4 · Quality Education
0%
Average Pass Rate Needs Targeted Intervention
Only 2 of 10 schools exceed the 70% GES benchmark. Northern and Upper East regions lag with rates below 55%. Curriculum support, teacher CPD and learning materials remain critical gaps requiring urgent policy action.
  • Deploy GES master teachers as cluster mentors in low-performing schools
  • Provide textbook packs aligned with BECE syllabus to all pupils
  • Introduce termly diagnostic assessments with remediation protocols
  • Incentivise qualified teachers with northern-region hardship allowances
SDG 5 · Gender Equality
0%
Girls Enrollment Below Parity in 4 Schools
Upper West, Upper East, Northern and Volta regions report girls' enrollment below 45%, indicating persistent socio-cultural barriers. Conditional cash transfers and safe-school initiatives could help close the gap rapidly.
  • Implement school-based girls' clubs and female role-model visits
  • Provide menstrual hygiene kits and private WASH facilities
  • Partner with district assemblies on conditional fee-waiver programmes
  • Engage community elders to reduce socio-cultural school-exit barriers
SDG 1 · No Poverty
₵310
Median Annual Fees Exclude Poorest Households
With Ghana's poorest quintile earning under ₵3,600/year, school fees of ₵310–₵460 represent 9–13% of household income. Scholarship schemes and flexible payment options are urgently needed to prevent dropout.
  • Introduce income-assessed sliding-scale fees across all 10 schools
  • Apply for LEAP (Livelihood Empowerment Against Poverty) programme linkages
  • Establish alumni donor fund for scholarship endowments
  • Partner with mobile money platforms for instalment payment options
SDG 4.a · Learning Environments
0
Infrastructure Critically Low in 5 Schools
Average infrastructure score is 47/100, with 5 schools below 45. Inadequate sanitation, absent libraries, and overcrowded classrooms directly reduce learning outcomes — especially for girls.
  • Prioritise WASH construction at Upper East and Upper West schools
  • Apply for GETFund grants for classroom block rehabilitation
  • Partner with local NGOs for library and reading corner installations
  • Develop annual infrastructure audit protocol for GES monitoring
Policy Recommendations Matrix
Three-Phase Action Plan for Ghana Education Service
Immediate (0–6 months)
Deploy teacher support clusters in Northern and Upper East regions.
Introduce school feeding supplements in below-55% pass-rate schools.
Conduct WASH audits at all 10 schools and remediate critical failures.
Launch girls' enrolment drive in 4 below-parity regions.
Medium-term (6–18 months)
Roll out conditional cash transfers for girls in parity-gap regions.
Partner with NGOs for library and ICT lab infrastructure grants.
Introduce tiered fee waivers linked to household income assessment.
Establish termly learning outcome monitoring dashboards.
Strategic (18 months+)
Integrate this dataset into Ghana's national EMIS for SDG 4 tracking.
Build public-private partnerships to co-fund teacher salaries.
Develop regional SDG scorecards for accountability reporting.
Scale successful pilots to all 10 regions with GES endorsement.
